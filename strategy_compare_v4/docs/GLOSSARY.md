# 2. Trading Terms

## Strategy

A predefined set of trading rules that generates buy and sell signals.

______________________________________________________________________

## Backtest

A simulation of a trading strategy using historical market data to estimate performance.

______________________________________________________________________

## Trade

A completed transaction consisting of an entry and an exit.

______________________________________________________________________

## Win Rate

The percentage of profitable trades.

**Formula**

```
Winning Trades / Total Trades × 100
```

______________________________________________________________________

## Average Win

The average profit generated by winning trades.

______________________________________________________________________

## Average Loss

The average loss incurred by losing trades.

______________________________________________________________________

# 3. Performance Metrics

## Expectancy

The average expected profit or loss per trade.

**Formula**

```
Expectancy =
(Win Rate × Average Win)
−
(Loss Rate × Average Loss)
```

Interpretation:

- Positive → profitable over time.
- Negative → losing over time.

______________________________________________________________________

## Profit Factor

Gross Profit divided by Gross Loss.

**Formula**

```
Profit Factor =
Gross Profit / Gross Loss
```

Typical interpretation:

- < 1.0 → Losing
- 1.5–2.0 → Good
- > 2.0 → Strong

______________________________________________________________________

## Reward Risk

Average winning trade divided by average losing trade.

A higher value indicates better trade quality.

______________________________________________________________________

## Profit Velocity

Profit generated per unit of time.

Higher values indicate more efficient capital utilization.

______________________________________________________________________

# 4. Risk Metrics

## Drawdown

The decline from a historical portfolio peak to a subsequent trough.

______________________________________________________________________

## Maximum Drawdown

The largest observed drawdown during the evaluation period.

______________________________________________________________________

## Stability

A measure of consistency in returns.

Higher stability suggests repeatable performance.

______________________________________________________________________

## Risk Score

A normalized score summarizing downside risk characteristics.

______________________________________________________________________

# 5. Institutional Scores

## Edge Score

Measures the sustainable trading advantage of a strategy.

Derived from metrics such as:

- Expectancy
- Profit Factor
- Reward Risk

______________________________________________________________________

## Reliability Score

Measures confidence in future performance.

Factors include:

- Trade count
- Stability
- Validation checks

______________________________________________________________________

## Efficiency Score

Measures how efficiently capital is employed.

Factors include:

- Holding Efficiency
- Signal Quality
- Opportunity Score

______________________________________________________________________

## Composite Score

The primary ranking metric combining Edge, Reliability, and Efficiency Scores.

It represents the overall institutional quality of a strategy.

______________________________________________________________________

# 6. Portfolio Terms

## Allocation

The percentage of capital assigned to a position.

______________________________________________________________________

## Position Weight

The fraction of total portfolio value invested in a security.

______________________________________________________________________

## Diversification

The practice of spreading investments to reduce concentration risk.

______________________________________________________________________

## Correlation

A statistical measure of how two strategies move relative to each other.

Values range from -1 to +1.

______________________________________________________________________

## Normalization

The process of scaling values so that portfolio weights sum to 100%.
